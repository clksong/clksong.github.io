I am an associate professor at the School of Data Science, The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen (CUHK-SZ). Before joining CUHS-SZ, I was the director of Sinovation Ventures AI Institute (Shenzhen) and worked in Microsoft and Tencent prior to Sinovation Ventures. Currently I am also an affiliate faculty at Department of Linguistics, University of Washington (UW), and the chief scientist at Sinovation Ventures AI Institute (Shenzhen).

My research intersts focus on natural language processing (NLP), especially text representation learning, language understanding and generation, information extraction and retrieval, etc. I am particularly interested in medical NLP, and studied for years on applying NLP to different medical scenarios. Many of my previous research have been transferred into products or open resources that facilitate academia and industry applications, e.g., Microsoft XiaoICE, Tencent Chinese word embeddings, etc. Recently my team has released a new Chinese pre-trained models (ZEN) and the related codes, which can be found here.

  • ZEN
    • ZEN is a BERT-based Chinese (Z) text encoder Enhanced by N-gram representations, where different combinations of characters are considered during training. The potential word or phrase boundaries are explicitly pre-trained and fine-tuned with the character encoder (BERT), so that ZEN incorporates the comprehensive information of both the character sequence and words or phrases it contains. Currently ZEN has been used in many Chinese NLP tasks and achieved the state-of-the-art performance, such as Chinese word segmentation and part-of-speech tagging. More details can be found at here.

  • WMSeg and TwASP
    • WMSeg and TwASP are two projects on Chinese word segmentation (CWS) and part-of-speech (POS) tagging, corresponding to our two papers in ACL-2020. We use memory networks and two-way attentions to integrate automatic knowledge such as n-grams and auto-generated parsing results to faciliate CWS and POS tagging. These projects can be used as references for sequence labeling tasks in NLP with knowledge incorporation. Details can be found in the GitHub projects: WMSeg and TwASP

  • DSG
    • This is the source code for training Tencent large scale Chinese word embeddings, with support of distinguishing left and right context when training skip-gram style word embeddings. I know a lot of people argue that why there is no GitHub project hosting this code :-), but actually it was released in the first place with my paper published in NAACL-2018.
